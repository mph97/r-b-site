The Problem

90s-00s Peak

One look at the Billboard charts of the 1990s through the mid-2000s will show that R&B was one of the most popular genres, if not the most popular. R&B *was* pop music and it wasn't uncommon for albums and songs to hold the top spots for weeks at a time. In the 90s, artists like Mariah Carey, Boyz II Men, Brandy, Usher, and SWV were able to find mainstream success with their music. During the early to mid-2000s, Usher, Ne-Yo, Mariah Carey, Mary J. Blige, and Alicia Keys, to name a few, were topping the charts and their music was inescapable.

Late 00s decline

Around 08-09, there was a shift in the sound of R&B. Some of the genre's most popular artists were incorporating Eurodance production into their music which was jarring to their audiences. At the same time, Hip-Hop was dominating. Albums such as Kanye West's '808s and Heartbreaks' proved to be a gamechanger with Wests singing over Electronic influenced Hip-Hop and R&B. This style influenced artists like Drake who broke through with his ability to fuse R&B sensibilites with his raps. Consumers naturally gravitated towards this new phenomenon happening and, as a result, R&B artists suffered. Artists had to adapt to a downtempo production style heavily influenced by hip-hop that favored a more drawn back approach to the traditional singing style most were used to.

Rise of "Alt-R&B"

During the 2010s, this new sound that had fully taken over R&B was dubbed "Alternative R&B". Frank Ocean, The Weeknd, and Jhene Aiko can be credited as the faces of the genre, mixing elements from multiple genres with introspective, sometimes stream-of-consciousness, type songwriting. For a while, their styles were replicated over and over again. Music listeners were satisfied enough with the music they were getting, but, for the most part, Hip-Hop's dominance as the leading genre had overshadowed nearly all of recent R&B releases.
